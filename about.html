About the Event

This is the third iteration of something I started four years ago. I've loved golf all my life, and I wanted to make a difference for those who are suffering and in serious need of help. I decided to use one of my favorite things in life to help facilitate that desire.

This year, join us for a round of golf as we honor my sister's life, Jennifer. She had a serene connection to people and animals. She was an empath like no other, with deep compassion for everyone around her.

Jennifer was bullied at a young age, which exacerbated her depression and anxiety as she grew older. Unfortunately, the prescriptions she received as a teenager did not help and instead led her to turn to substances while searching for answers.

In 2022, my sister got sober from opiates. During that time, she built a meaningful outreach on social media to help others suffering from addiction. She supported people during their most painful moments, and without a doubt, she saved lives through her compassion.

As often happens, negativity eventually followed the positive impact she made. Jennifer experienced severe harassment online, and old habits returned, creating an avalanche that could not be stopped.

Jennifer passed away peacefully in our living room on February 28, 2023.

In honor of the lives she touched and saved, this year’s fundraiser supports the Savage Sisters Organization — a 501(c)(3) nonprofit providing trauma-informed recovery housing, disease prevention, and statewide education on substance use disorder.

In 2024, Savage Sisters reached 39,740 people, distributed 13,344 naloxone doses, and conducted 721 outreaches across the state.

My sister was a Savage — and so are they. Let’s raise money, smash drives, and save lives, together.
