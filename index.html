Biyearly Golf Fundraiser

Supporting Savage Sisters in 2026 in memory of Jennifer Killea

About the Event

My third iteration of something I started four years ago. I've loved golf all my life. I wanted to make a difference to those out there that are suffering and are in serious need of help. So, I decided to use one of my favorite things in life to facilitate this burning desire that I have.

This year, join us in a round of golf as we honor my sister's life, Jennifer. My sister had a serene connection to people and animals. She was an empath like no other, and had so much passion for everyone around her.

Jennifer was bullied at a young age, and this exacerbated her depression and anxiety as she grew up. Unfortunately, the strength and the age at which she was prescribed medications did not help.

My sister got sober from opiates in 2022. Upon doing so, she accomplished a large outreach on social media to others who were currently suffering from addiction. She was there for them when they were in pain, and needed someone by their side. Without a doubt, she saved lives with her outreach.

Then, social media did its thing. Negativity caught up to the positive impact she made, and Jennifer experienced a great deal of harassment. Old habits returned, creating an avalanche that could not be stopped.

Jennifer passed away peacefully in our living room on February 28, 2023.

In honoring the lives she saved, this year I am fundraising for the Savage Sisters Organization. They are a 501(c)(3) nonprofit providing trauma-informed recovery housing, disease prevention, and statewide education on substance use disorder.

In 2024, Savage Sisters reached 39,740 people, distributed 13,344 naloxone doses, and conducted 721 outreaches.

My sister was a Savage — and so are they. Let’s raise money, save lives, and hit 300-yard drives.
